Making a video ad used to mean either hiring a production team or piecing together footage in a heavyweight editor. The workflow below strips it down to five decisions, starting footage, hook, product demo, music, and export, that you can move through in one sitting.

What do you need before making a video ad?

Before opening any tool, decide the ad's angle (what emotional hook or product moment it's built around). Most ads on UGCDrop combine a library clip (the hook/reaction footage) with your own product demo footage in the same render, so you're not choosing one over the other, you're picking which library clip pairs best with the product footage you have.

  • Your ad angle: A reaction/testimonial style, a straight product demo, or a before/after
  • A library clip: Real or AI-generated UGC-style footage to open the ad or carry the reaction
  • Your product demo clip: Footage of the actual product, even a short one, so the ad ties back to what you're selling
  • A hook: The line or on-screen text that has to land in the first 1-3 seconds

Step 1: Pick a library clip to pair with your product footage

In UGCDrop's Studio, the standard build combines both: a library clip (real or AI-generated) for the hook or reaction, cut into your own uploaded product demo footage a few seconds in. That's exactly why the Studio has a separate Library tab and Demos tab, they're meant to be used together, not as alternatives.

If you'd rather not pull from the library at all, the Custom Videos option ($1/video) gets you a clip made specifically for your product instead.

How do you build the ad step by step?

In UGCDrop's Studio, the build order is: add a hook, pick a base clip from the library, attach your own product demo, layer in music, then render. Each render spends one Studio Credit.

1
Add a hook
In the Hooks tab, paste a text hook, one per line if you want to generate multiple video variants at once. No hook ideas? The built-in AI hook generator (powered by Claude) can produce viral-style hooks for your specific product.
2
Choose your base footage
In the Library tab, pick a clip from the 100,000+ B-roll and creator-clip library that matches your angle, filtering by emotion, setting, age, gender, and style.
3
Attach your product demo
In the Demos tab, upload footage of your actual product so the ad ties back to what you're selling, not just a generic reaction clip.
4
Add music
In the Music tab, add a background track to set pacing and tone.
5
Render, download, and launch
Rendering spends a Studio Credit. The finished video is kept for 30 days before automatic deletion, download it and launch it as an ad on your platform of choice within that window.
