GridBank is a UGC clip sourcing platform built around a library of camera-roll-style footage, sold through a prepaid quarterly subscription rather than the monthly or pay-as-you-go plans common elsewhere in the category. Because the pricing model is unusual, "GridBank pricing" is one of the more searched questions about the platform. Here's the full, honest breakdown of what it costs, including what isn't publicly disclosed.

What is GridBank?

GridBank is a UGC clip sourcing platform that gives marketers access to a library of raw, camera-roll-style video footage for use in ads and social content. Instead of commissioning creators individually, users subscribe to GridBank's library and download clips against their plan's allowance.

It competes in the same category as other UGC clip libraries, the pitch is authentic, unscripted footage without the cost or turnaround of hiring individual creators. The differentiator in GridBank's case is its pricing structure, which is billed quarterly rather than monthly.

How much does GridBank cost?

GridBank's only publicly listed pricing tier is the Quarterly Plan, priced at $890 per quarter and including 120 downloads for that period. That works out to roughly $7.41 per clip. GridBank also lists an Agency Plan, but does not publish a price for it.

⚠
No published Agency pricing
GridBank does not publish an exact price for its Agency Plan. Based on how it's positioned, it's most likely a custom, volume-based enterprise arrangement negotiated directly with GridBank rather than a fixed listed rate, treat any specific number you see elsewhere as unverified.

Is there a GridBank free trial?

GridBank does not publicly list a free trial. Its published pricing structure consists solely of the prepaid Quarterly Plan and the custom Agency Plan, there's no mention of a free tier or trial period in what it discloses publicly.

Does GridBank charge per clip or per download?

GridBank does not sell individual clips or single downloads as a standalone purchase. Access is bundled entirely into the $890/quarter subscription, which includes an allotment of 120 downloads for that billing period, there is no publicly listed pay-as-you-go or per-clip option.

  • No monthly billing: GridBank's published plan bills quarterly, not monthly
  • No single-clip purchases: You can't buy one download at a time, the 120-download allowance comes bundled with the quarterly commitment
  • Prepaid structure: The full $890 is paid upfront for the quarter, regardless of how many of the 120 downloads you actually use

What to check before committing to any quarterly plan
Before prepaying for a quarter of downloads on any platform, estimate how many clips you'll realistically use in that window. Unused downloads on a prepaid plan are sunk cost, a monthly or pay-as-you-go model avoids that risk entirely.
