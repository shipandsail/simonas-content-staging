What is Arcads and how does it work?

Arcads is an AI video ad platform that generates talking-head videos using AI actors based on real human footage. You write a script, pick an actor from a library of 300+, preview the voice, and generate a finished video — the actor's lips and voice sync to your script while their body language and environment remain real.

The workflow is primarily text-to-video: you input a script, Arcads renders the AI actor speaking it. A speech-to-speech mode also lets you upload audio or record directly in the browser — Arcads converts it to the actor's voice. Credits are not spent until after you preview the actor's voice, which prevents wasting budget on a voice that doesn't fit.

1
Write your script
Type your ad script directly, upload audio, or record in the browser. Arcads' hook generator tool helps with angle ideas. No built-in AI script generation — write externally using Claude or ChatGPT.
2
Select your AI actors
Browse 300+ AI actors filtered by gender, age group, skin tone, and environment. Multiple actors can be added to one script — generate several videos in a single batch for creative testing.
3
Preview the voice before generating
Listen to how each actor's voice sounds with your script before spending any credits. This preview step is one of Arcads' strongest workflow features — no wasted renders on voices that don't fit.
4
Generate and download
Arcads processes the video (a few minutes per render). Download the finished clip and take it to your editing tool of choice — CapCut, Premiere, or similar — to add graphics, captions, and product footage.

What are the pros and cons of Arcads?

The main strength of Arcads is actor realism — its AI actors are consistently rated among the most lifelike available, trained on real human footage rather than computer-generated characters. The main weakness is scope: Arcads handles only the actor clip itself, requiring external tools for scripting, editing, and physical product demonstration.

What Arcads does well

  • 300+ actors trained on real humans — among the most realistic AI actors available
  • Voice preview before spending credits — no wasted renders
  • Batch generation — multiple actors from one script in a single run
  • Hook generator tool for creative angle inspiration
  • Cheaper and faster than hiring a UGC creator ($11/video vs $80-200+ on Fiverr)
  • Speech-to-speech input — upload audio or record in-browser
  • Managed editing service available (2-day turnaround via call)

Where Arcads falls short

  • No built-in script generation — must write scripts externally
  • No video editing tools — requires CapCut or similar for final production
  • AI actors cannot hold or interact with physical products
  • Pricing hidden — not shown publicly, only revealed during onboarding
  • No free trial — no way to test output before committing to $110/month
  • Bare-bones UI with no onboarding walkthrough
  • Limited use for physical ecommerce without significant additional editing
⚠
Physical Product Limitation
Arcads AI actors cannot hold, use, or interact with real products. For ecommerce brands where showing the product in use is central to the ad, Arcads contributes only voiceover or overlay — the product demonstration must come from separate footage. This is the platform's most significant limitation for physical product advertisers.

Who is Arcads actually best for?

Arcads is best for businesses selling digital products — SaaS, courses, coaching programs, newsletters, templates, and services — where the ad does not need to visually demonstrate a physical product. For digital products, an AI actor speaking directly to camera can represent up to 80% of the ad content, making Arcads a highly efficient production tool.

When does Arcads work well?

  • SaaS and apps: Screen recordings or UI demos can be overlaid on the AI actor clip — no physical product required.
  • Online courses and coaching: An AI presenter can deliver value propositions and testimonial-style ads credibly.
  • Newsletter and subscription growth: Talking-head ads work natively for driving sign-ups to digital content.
  • Agencies testing creative at volume: Batch generation lets agencies produce 10+ actor variations from one script for client testing.

When does Arcads fall short?

  • Physical ecommerce products: Skincare, supplements, gadgets, clothing — any product that needs to be shown in use cannot be demonstrated by an AI actor.
  • Dropshipping: Dropshippers need authentic product reaction hooks paired with supplier footage. Arcads actors cannot react to or hold real products.
  • High-volume hook testing: At $11/video and 10 videos per $110/month, testing 20-30 hook variations per product is expensive. UGCDrop provides 30 real human clips for $28/month.
