Why is the price spread so wide?

These tools aren't competing for the same job. Splitting them by what they actually do explains most of the price gap:

Scheduling tools (cheapest)

Buffer ($6/mo per channel) and Planoly ($14/mo) assume you already have a working account and just need a calendar to queue posts. They connect via official platform APIs and don't touch account warming, region targeting, or account sourcing at all.

TikTok-specific growth suites (mid-range)

GenViral ($29/mo) and TokUpgrade ($49/mo) add TikTok-specific features on top of scheduling, think content tools or growth analytics geared at TikTok specifically rather than general social media.

Account sourcing & multi-account management (highest)

TokPortal ($49-$595/mo, credit-based) and HardLaunch ($999/mo minimum) are built for agencies running fleets of accounts across countries, with warming, device infrastructure, and analytics bundled in. Tikomate ($25 one-time) covers the narrower job of getting one verified US account without the recurring agency-scale overhead.

💡
The real question isn't "which is cheapest"
It's "what am I actually trying to do." If you need a scheduling calendar for an account you already have, Buffer or Planoly at $6-14/mo wins easily. If you need to get a legitimate US-registered account and don't want a subscription, Tikomate's $25 one-time is the lowest-commitment path. If you're running dozens of accounts for an agency, TokPortal or HardLaunch's higher monthly cost buys infrastructure the cheaper tools don't offer at all.

Which tool should you actually pick?

  • Just need to schedule posts on an account you already own: Buffer or Planoly.
  • Need one legitimate US TikTok account without a subscription: Tikomate ($25 one-time).
  • Want TikTok-specific growth tooling on top of an existing account: GenViral or TokUpgrade.
  • Running an agency managing many accounts across countries: TokPortal or HardLaunch, budget accordingly for the monthly floor.
