Why does a VPN not give you a US TikTok account?

This is the most common mistake international creators make. Using a VPN to get a US IP address and then creating a TikTok account does not reliably result in a US-registered account. TikTok cross-checks multiple signals to determine account location at registration:

  • SIM card country: The mobile network your phone is connected to at registration is one of TikTok's primary location signals.
  • Device registration history: Your device's prior TikTok activity and location data is factored in.
  • IP address at registration: A VPN IP is detectable by TikTok's systems as a proxy or data center IP in many cases.
  • App store region: The country of your Apple ID or Google Play account also signals location.

Even if a VPN-created account initially appears to register as US-based, TikTok's systems may re-classify it later — removing access to Creator Rewards and TikTok Shop. Pre-verified accounts avoid this entirely because they are set up correctly from the start.
