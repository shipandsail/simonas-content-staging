VidCloner and Creatify are both AI video tools aimed at creators and brands posting on TikTok — but they solve completely different problems. Creatify starts from a product URL and generates new AI avatar ads. VidCloner starts from a viral video URL and clones it with your face. If you are comparing the two, the decision usually comes down to one question: do you want to generate new videos from scratch, or do you want to clone content that already proven it works?

What is the main difference between VidCloner and Creatify?

The core difference is the starting point. Creatify generates new videos from your product — you input a product URL, choose a stock AI avatar, and Creatify builds an ad creative for you. VidCloner starts from existing content — you paste the URL of any viral TikTok or Instagram video and VidCloner replaces the actor with your custom avatar, matching the exact movements, timing, and energy of the original.

Go Deeper: Side-by-Side Breakdown

1. How videos are created: viral URL cloning vs. AI generation from scratch

VidCloner's workflow starts with content that has already earned traction on the algorithm. You copy a TikTok or Instagram Reel URL, paste it into VidCloner, select your custom avatar, and the platform clones the video — same pacing, same gestures, same energy, new face. The risk is near-zero: you are not gambling on whether a new concept will perform, you are replicating a format the algorithm has already rewarded.

Creatify's workflow starts from zero. You input a product URL, Creatify scrapes the listing, chooses a stock avatar and script template, and builds a new video from scratch. The output is fast and scalable, but you are generating new content each time — and not every AI-generated ad creative will perform. High-volume testing is Creatify's strength, which is why it attracts e-commerce brands running paid campaigns on Meta and TikTok Ads.

2. Avatar type: your custom personal brand vs. stock AI presenters

Creatify's Starter plan gives access to 300 stock AI avatars — polished, diverse, and ready to use immediately. The Pro plan expands that to 1,500 avatars. These are pre-built characters: they look convincing but they are not you, and audiences building a relationship with a personal brand can tell the difference.

VidCloner generates a custom avatar from your own footage. You upload a short clip of yourself and VidCloner creates a digital double that matches your unique body language, voice, and mannerisms. Every cloned video looks like it was filmed by you personally — because the movements and timing come from your real appearance. For creators running a TikTok presence or TikTok Shop storefront, this authenticity advantage compounds over time.

3. Pricing and credit model: flat-rate vs. expiring credits

VidCloner uses a flat-rate model. Starter is $19/month for 5 video clones. Creator is $49/month for 15. Master is $59/month for 30. You always know exactly what you get each month, and unused credits do not affect your plan — the count resets on renewal.

Creatify uses a credit system where credits expire at the end of each billing cycle. The number of videos you can produce depends on which AI model and avatar type you use — variable credit costs per video make it harder to predict monthly output. Brands with fluctuating content schedules often find they either lose unused credits during slow months or scramble to use them up before renewal.
