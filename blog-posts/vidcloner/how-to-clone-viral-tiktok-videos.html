What does "cloning a viral TikTok video" mean?

In TikTok marketing, cloning a viral video means recreating the exact movements, timing, and format of a successful video, replacing the original creator with your own face or AI avatar. The cloned video inherits the proven structure of the original while delivering your own content, product, or brand message.

Cloning is distinct from dueting or remixing. A duet places two videos side by side. A remix uses the original audio. A clone replaces the creator entirely, same gestures, same pacing, same hook structure, entirely new face and context. The result behaves like original content in the TikTok algorithm because it technically is original content.

  • Format cloning: Copy the hook timing, movement, and transition structure of a viral video
  • Avatar replacement: Your face, your AI avatar, or a custom digital character performs in place of the original creator
  • Content swapping: Change the product, message, or call to action while keeping the viral format intact

Is cloning legal?
Cloning a video's format and movement structure is legal, you are not copying footage, audio, or the creator's likeness. You are recreating a concept with your own avatar. Directly reposting someone else's original video without permission is copyright infringement. Format cloning with your own content is not.
