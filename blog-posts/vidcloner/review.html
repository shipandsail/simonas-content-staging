VidCloner's Weaknesses: Where It Falls Short

1. High Dependency on Baseline Quality

VidCloner's renders are only as good as the baseline. If you upload a training video with echoey audio or dim lighting, the cloned avatar will exhibit robotic mouth artifacts. You must record a clean initial 2-minute raw file.

2. Render Queues During Peak Hours

Since rendering high-definition avatars is GPU-heavy, VidCloner's processing queues can swell to 10 minutes during peak US business hours. Standard plans have no queue bypass.

3. Credit Consumption for Re-renders

If you generate a video and notice a typo in your script, re-rendering the updated script consumes credits. Standard plans must budget credits carefully during creative testing.
