Creatify and HeyGen are both AI-avatar ad generators aimed at e-commerce brands, but they differ in starting point and pricing model. Creatify starts from your product URL; HeyGen starts from a stock presenter library. This guide breaks down which is the better fit.

What is the main difference between Creatify and HeyGen?

Creatify scrapes your product listing and builds an ad around a stock AI avatar, backed by a competitor ad database analyzing 30M+ ads for reference. HeyGen instead offers a much larger stock presenter library (300-1,500 avatars) plus ad launcher integrations for pushing campaigns directly into Meta and AppLovin.

Go Deeper: Side-by-Side Breakdown

1. Starting Point: Product URL vs Stock Script

Creatify's workflow begins with your product listing, it scrapes the page and builds an ad around it automatically. HeyGen requires you to write a script manually and choose a presenter, giving more creative control at the cost of speed.

2. Ad Intelligence: Both Offer Competitor Databases

Both tools include a competitor ad intelligence layer, Creatify analyzes 30M+ ads, HeyGen's Pro plan bundles similar research tools alongside its ad launcher. Neither is a clear leader here; the difference comes down to how each surfaces the data inside the editor.

3. Pricing and Credits: Expiring Credits vs Tiered Plans

Creatify's Starter plan is $39/month for 100 credits that expire at the end of each billing cycle, unused credits do not roll over. HeyGen's Creator plan is $29/month with credit-based rendering, and its Pro plan at $49/month unlocks custom avatars and ad intelligence. Both rely on stock avatars either way, neither lets you feature your own face.
